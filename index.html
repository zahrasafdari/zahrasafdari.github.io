About Me

Hi there!


I'm Zahra Safdari, a Computer Science student at the University of Tabriz, set to graduate next year. My deep passion lies in the realm of artificial intelligence, and I'm particularly focused on harnessing its capabilities to tackle real-world challenges, with a specific emphasis on Natural Language Processing (NLP). Currently, I am privileged to serve as a research assistant, working under the guidance of Dr. Rashid Behzadidoost at the University of Tabriz.


In response to the alarming surge in offensive and hate speech across social media platforms, our team has conceived an innovative solution. Our project revolves around the utilization of Contrastive Learning techniques, combined with Transformer-based models, to detect hate speech across seven distinct languages


My journey into the world of NLP began during my second year of undergraduate studies, and my enduring fascination with linguistics led me to merge this field with the power of AI. Each day, I delve deeper into the nuances of NLP, which serves as a constant source of motivation, propelling me to strive for optimal solutions to the challenges that surround us.
