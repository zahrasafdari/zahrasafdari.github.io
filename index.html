About Me

Hello! I'm Arash Akbari, a third-year student at the University of Tehran. I really enjoy finding smart solutions to difficult problems that involve data. I'm really interested in things like computers understanding images, making robots work, and controlling systems. I'm also fascinated by the idea of making things secure even when they're being attacked by others.


I like coming up with new ideas to solve tricky problems that involve data. I'm most excited about computer vision, robotics, and control systems. These topics really inspire me, and I've been exploring them a lot through research.


I got started with computer vision during an internship. That experience got me interested in using computers to understand what they see. I worked on making a computer program that can grab objects, and I used special tools to help me. I learned a lot about this and wrote a paper about what I found.


Presently, I'm at The CISPA Helmholtz Center for Information Security. I'm learning a lot about how to make computer programs that understand images more secure, even when they're being attacked. This internship is really helping me learn more and contribute to the field of information security.
